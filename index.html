About Perseus

  • Before Perseus was born, Acrisius the King of Argos was given a prophecy that one day one of Danae's sons would kill him
  • To prevent this, Acrisius locked Danae up in a chamber with no doors so that she would never have any children
  • Zeus grew fond of Danae, appeared to her in the form of golden rain, and impregnated her
  • After Perseus was born, Acrisius locked Danae and the child in a wooden chest and threw the chest in the ocean
  • The chest washed up safely on the island of Seriphos, and Perseus was saved by Dictys and grew up under his care
